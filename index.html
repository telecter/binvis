In decimal (base 10), you would count in the powers of 10. For example,

162

hundreds or 102 place..1
tens or 101 place......6
ones, or 100 place.....2

So then, to find your number, multiply the numbers by their place value:

102(1) +
101(6) +
100(2) = 162

It works the same in binary,

10100010

Counting in the powers of two instead of ten, each bit to the left has more value.

20 place...0
21 place...1
22 place...0
...and so on to the left

By adding them up just like in base 10, you can convert the binary number back.

20(0) + 21(1) + 22(0) + 23(0) + 24(0) + 25(1) + 26(0) + 27(1) = 162

This gives us 162, which is 10100010 represented in decimal.
